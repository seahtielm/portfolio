My Favorite TV Shows and Films

Discover the television series and movies that have captivated me with their storytelling, characters, and visual aesthetics.

When Life gives you Tangerines

Directors: Kim Won-Suk

As mentioned before, this is also known as "You Have Done Well". Short Synopsis: This drama tells the story of Ae-Soon and Gwan-Sik, born on Jeju Island in the 1950s, following their adventurous lives. It is a coming of age story.

Cast: IU (Lee Ji-eun) as Ae-Soon, Park Bo-gum as Gwan-Sik

Last One Laughing Philippines

Director: Jay Klio Bermudez

LOL: Last One Laughing Philippines is a Philippine comedy reality competition show based on Documental, a format created by Japanese comedian Hitoshi Matsumoto. Hosted by comedian, presenter, and actor Vice Ganda, it aired on Prime Video on July 4, 2024.

Cast: Chad Kinis, Jerald Napoles, Pepe Herrera, Victor Anastacio, Empoy Marquez, Tuesday Vargas, Rufa Mae Quinto, Kim Molina, Jayson Gainza

Ang Mutya ng Section E

Director: Theodore Boborol

It follows 16 rowdy guys and a fierce young woman from Section E. Their lives become chaotic when Jayjay Mariano, a talented singer and former Ppop Generation member, joins the class as the "mutya" of Section E—the lowest and most unruly section in Higher Value International School.

Cast:Ashtine Olviga as Jasper Jean "Jayjay" Mariano, Andres Muhlach as Kiefer Watson, Rabin Angeles as Yuri Hanamitchi

Incognito

Director: Pimentel Ong

Synopsis In search of redemption, a group of seven misfits, seeking redemption, join a private military company led by Gregorio "Greg" Paterno (Ian Veneracion), on a covert group undertakes deadly missions targeting the criminal underworld, specifically focusing on human trafficking issues that the government can't address directly.

Cast: Richard Gutierrez as Jose "JB" Bonifacio / Shark, Ian Veneracion as Col. Gregorio "Greg" Paterno / Contractor / Noah, Baron Geisler as Miguel "Migs" Tecson / Jaguar, Kaila Estrada as Max Alvero / Eagle / Metamorphosis, Anthony Jennings as Tomas "Tom" Guerrero / Monkey, Maris Racal as Gabriela "Gab" Rivera / Sparrow, Daniel Padilla as Andres Malvar / Wolf

50 First Dates

Director: Peter Segal

Short Synopsis: Henry Roth, a veterinarian living in Hawaii, has a fear of commitment until he meets Lucy Whitmore, a charming art teacher. However, Lucy suffers from short-term memory loss, forgetting everything that happened the previous day. Henry must win her heart anew each day.

Cast: Adam Sandler (Henry Roth), Drew Barrymore (Lucy Whitmore), Rob Schneider (Ula)

Just Go with It

Director: Dennis Dugan

Short Synopsis: Danny Maccabee, a successful plastic surgeon, avoids commitment by pretending to be unhappily married. When he meets the woman of his dreams, he enlists his loyal assistant, Katherine, to pose as his soon-to-be ex-wife. Complications and romantic entanglements ensue.

Cast: Adam Sandler (Danny Maccabee), Jennifer Aniston (Katherine Murphy), Nicole Kidman (Devlin Adams)

Annabelle

Director: John R. Leonetti

Short Synopsis: Before the events of "The Conjuring," John Form finds the perfect gift for his expecting wife, Mia—a beautiful, rare vintage doll named Annabelle. But Mia's delight with Annabelle doesn't last long. On one horrific night, their home is invaded by satanic cult members, who violently attack the couple. Bloodshed and terror are not all the cultists leave behind. The cultists conjure a malicious entity that is now attached to the Annabelle doll.

Cast: Annabelle Wallis (Mia Form), Ward Horton (John Form), Alfre Woodard (Evelyn)

Charlie's Angel

Director: McG

Short Synopsis: Three highly skilled female private investigators, known as "Angels," are hired to rescue a kidnapped software genius and prevent his revolutionary voice identification software from falling into the wrong hands. They use their martial arts skills, high-tech gadgets, and seductive charm to complete their mission.

Cast: Cameron Diaz (Natalie Cook), Drew Barrymore (Dylan Sanders), Lucy Liu (Alex Munday)
